## рџ”¬ JDII Score Methodology

The JDII Score is a **weighted composite** of 5 inflation signals, derived from Pearson correlation analysis:

| Factor | Weight | Correlation with JDII |
|---|---|---|
| Experience Required (yrs) | **40%** | r = 0.89 |
| Skills Listed (count) | **30%** | r = 0.86 |
| Responsibilities Count | **15%** | r = 0.78 |
| Certifications Required | **10%** | r = 0.28 |
| Buzzword Count | **5%** | r = 0.19 |

Score range: **22 (realistic) в†’ 100 (extreme inflation)**

### рџЋЇ JDII Interpretation Scale

| Score Range | Inflation Level | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **22 вЂ“ 40** | рџџў Low Inflation | Realistic JD вЂ” experience and skills match the actual role |
| **41 вЂ“ 70** | рџџЎ Moderate Inflation | Some credential or skill padding, but still reasonable |
| **71 вЂ“ 85** | рџџ  High Inflation | Noticeably over-specified; candidate pool will be small |
| **86 вЂ“ 100** | рџ”ґ Extreme Inflation | Severely unrealistic; likely a barrier JD or wishlist posting |

> **2025 average JDII = 86.8** вЂ” the market has crossed into Extreme Inflation territory.

### Weight Justification

Weights were assigned in direct proportion to Pearson correlation strength with observed JDII inflation patterns across 8 years of data. Experience requirements (r = 0.89) and skills count (r = 0.86) showed the strongest linear relationship with inflation, accounting for 70% of the composite score. Responsibilities count (r = 0.78) was weighted at 15% as a moderately strong signal. Certifications (r = 0.28) and buzzword count (r = 0.19) are weaker but still statistically meaningful indicators of credential and language inflation respectively, and together contribute the final 15%.

## рџ“€ Key Findings

|---|---|---|---|

### 6 Key Insights
1. **JDII rose 29.2%** from 67.2 в†’ 86.8 вЂ” confirmed by linear regression (RВІ=0.83, p<0.05)
2. **Experience inflation is near-perfect linear** вЂ” RВІ=0.998, slope +0.28 yrs/year, p<0.001
3. **94% of postings require a post-grad degree** вЂ” MBA alone covers 45%; Bachelor's only 6%
4. **Remote jobs are statistically more inflated** than onsite (82.66 vs 78.37, t=9.78, p<0.001)
5. **Python demand grew from 41.8% в†’ 54.6%** вЂ” fastest growing skill in the dataset
6. **Forecast: JDII approaches 99.8 by 2028** вЂ” near saturation of the 100-point scale

## вљ пёЏ Limitations

This project is transparent about its boundaries:

- **Synthetic dataset** вЂ” Data was computationally generated, not scraped from real job boards. Patterns are directionally realistic but not empirically sourced.
- **Correlation в‰  causation** вЂ” Regression models identify trends; they do not prove causal mechanisms behind inflation.
- **Forecast assumes linearity** вЂ” The 2026вЂ“2028 forecasts assume historical linear trends continue unchanged, which may not hold.
- **JDII is a custom metric** вЂ” It is not an industry standard. Comparisons with external benchmarks require adaptation.
- **Static skill taxonomy** вЂ” The skills dictionary was fixed at model creation time; emerging tools (e.g., new AI frameworks) may not be captured.
- **No geographic segmentation** вЂ” Job location and regional market conditions are not factored into the JDII calculation.
